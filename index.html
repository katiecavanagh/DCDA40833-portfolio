Mid-Semester Reflection

What I learned: In Lab 1, I got a refresher on basic coding skills, including how to embed photos and change the size of them. Lab 2 focused on exploring different AI platforms. I used Adobe Firefly for image creation and Claude for writing, which helped me learn how to analyze different AI platforms and understand what they do best. In Lab 3, I learned how to analyze visual art data. I realized that there were metaphors and deeper meaning behind the art, and that there are different levels of accessibility within these art data sets depending on how clearly the information is presented. In Lab 4, I learned how to create maps in Tableau using data sets. It was fun to customize my visuals and choose something I was interested in, and it was cool to see my work come to life. Lab 5 was also really fun because we learned that we could make our own video game using AI vibe coding. I learned how to use Visual Studio Code’s AI assistant to fix bugs and add CSS animations. In Lab 6, I learned that I can make my own interactive custom map and that the process was easier than I expected. I also learned how to make design choices and troubleshoot problems with the help of AI tools.

Challenges I overcame: Throughout the labs, I faced several challenges but learned how to work through them. In Lab 1, asking questions was really helpful, whether that was to my professor or classmates. In Lab 2, it was sometimes difficult to get the AI platforms to do exactly what I wanted, especially since it was early in the semester and I still had some bugs within my coding. In Lab 3, one challenge was interpreting the data and thinking about how other people might interpret it as well, not just how I personally saw it. For Lab 4, I was absent the day we went through the Tableau tutorial, so getting started was definitely a challenge. I ended up asking my peers for help and they were very kind in helping me get started. In Lab 5, I learned that staying persistent with the AI agent was important. I realized I needed to be very specific in my prompts, like making sure to say that changes should “apply to all HTML pages.” I also worked through glitches and differences between my project and my classmates’ projects. In Lab 6, I had to overcome relying too much on AI assistance. When I used the AI chat to help with colors and design elements, it accidentally applied colors to my entire website instead of just the map. I had to go back and fix that so everything looked consistent.

How I grew: Over the semester, my understanding of digital culture and data analytics grew a lot. At the beginning, coding and working with digital tools felt pretty rusty to me. As I worked through the labs, I became more comfortable experimenting, troubleshooting, and learning from mistakes when things didn’t work right away. I also started thinking more about how data and technology shape the way information is shared online. Through projects like data visualizations and interactive maps, I learned that design choices and presentation can make a big difference in how people understand information.

Looking ahead: In the future, I think these skills will be helpful when working with digital media, marketing, and communication. Knowing how to analyze data, create visuals, and understand how digital tools work will help me create more engaging and thoughtful content. Even if I’m not coding every day, having this experience helps me better understand the technology and data that power a lot of the digital platforms we use today.
